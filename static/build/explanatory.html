Explanatory Statement

Project ID: 27885

Project Title: Interactive Haskell Type Inference Exploration

You are invited to take part in this study. Please read this Explanatory Statement in full before deciding whether or not to participate in this research. If you would like further information regarding any aspect of this project, you are encouraged to contact the researchers via the phone numbers or email addresses listed above.

What does the research involve?

This study is designed to study the effeteness of different mode of error message in Chameleon. Chameleon is a tool we develop to help programers identify and solve Haskell type errors.

Once you navigated to the user study website, you will be prompted with 8 different code challenges. Each code challenge is an editable code fragment in a web page, and a debugging area on the right side of the webpage to help you investigate the type errors (if there are any). For half of the challenges, you will be assisted by Chameleon Basic Mode, and Chameleon Interactive Mode for the other half. At the top of the webpage you will see a banner indicating exactly which debugging system you are using.

After the study, you will be ask to leave an overall feedback of your experience in working with GHC and Chameleon debugger.

How do we collect the data?

We will collect the data in two methods:

  • 1. Through post-study web form.
  • 2. Through tele-metrics during the time you working on the challenges. This include tracking important timings (e.g. when you start and complete a certain challenge), and usage patterns (e.g. how many time do you consult the error message before making an edit) in your web browser.

We will use first party cookie (under the same domain name) to store a temporary unique identifier in your browser. We will not share this cookie to any other parties. Nor will we access any other cookie you already have installed from other websites. All data transmission from your browser to our server are encrypted.

Why were you chosen for this research?

You opt in to participate this research by click the link we posted on internet forum and interest group websites.

Consenting to participate in the project and withdrawing from the research

The consent process includes the following steps:

  • Navigate to the consent web page
  • Read the information about the data we collect, how we collect and how can you stop participate
  • Click "I understand the data collection and privacy implication and want to participate in this study" button

You can choose to withdraw from further participation at any stage. To withdraw from this study, you simply close the user study website tab in the web browser. We will erase all the data associate with your session if you did not complete the study. Additionally, we will provide you a withdraw ID at the start of this study. You can email the student investigator (shuai.fu@monash.edu) with your withdraw ID at anytime to withdraw your data, even if you completed the study.

Possible benefits and risks to participants

There's no risks involved in participating this research. You are not required to be physically present. The study does not require you to perform physical activities or interact with physical objects. We will keep the study within a minimal time frame of 20 minutes to avoid strain. The information you will provide in the study are limited to the area of programming language knowledge and skill.

The user study gives no immediate benefit to the participants, However in the long-term, you may benefit from the tools that we develop as part of the research. Broadly, this user study will help researchers to better understand how to design, and improve the compiler interface and interactive IDE.

Confidentiality

We will not collect any data that can be linked to your identity, your device, or your location. We will use a random identifier (such as GUID) to group the collected data by each participant.

All published data will remain anonymous. The collected data and questionnaire answers will be used in the publication.

The members of the this project may include the user study results in journal articles, conference papers, or book chapters. Student co-investigator may include the user study result in his Ph.D. thesis.

Storage of data

The data will be stored offline-only, in an encrypted hard drive located in Australia. The members of the Interactive Haskell Type Inference Exploration project will be able to access the data. The data will be erased from the hard drive 12 months after it is collected.
